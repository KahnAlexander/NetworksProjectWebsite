CS 499 Semester Project - Alex Kahn, Kellen Ross, Connor Schwirian

A descriptive analysis of food options near colleges and universities across the United States.

Project Introduction

For our research project, the initial question we wanted to answer was if there is a correlation between the quality of school a student attends to the quality of food available to those students. If we found that there was a correlation, was it directly related to the school levels being affected by income level? This project will show if lower-level college campuses are targeted by lower-cost fast food chains, and if higher-level schools are more surrounded by higher quality food establishments.

Project Plan

To answer this question, our team gathered data based on two different categories: school levels and food quality. The first category is discrete with values of:

  • Community College
  • State University
  • Private University
  • Ivy League

    • And the second category is also discrete wth values:
  • Fast Food
  • Sitdown Restaurant
  • Grocery Store
    •
    The other metrics we chose to measure by were the distance from a campus to some food establishment, chosen from a list of entries in the three food categories, and lists of school campuses compiled for each level.

    Global metric

    This metric shows what proportion of the overall list of requests were found near that campus. This globally compares campuses' scores in categories to each other, based on who found a certain ratio of the request list.

    Local metric


    This metric shows what proportion of a food category near a campus is made up of each category. This locally measures scores, finding what percentage of food businesses near a campus are made up of a category.
