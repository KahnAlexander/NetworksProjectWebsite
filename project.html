CS 499 Semester Project - Alex Kahn, Kellen Ross, Connor Schwirian

A descriptive analysis of food options near colleges and universities across the United States.

Analysis Overview

For our research we used the distances from college campuses to different food service establishments. We collected the data through the Google Maps Distance API. Distances were calculated by the length of a path from the Google Maps location of a campus to the address of a particular establishment. We made use of the Google Maps API to retrieve lists of local establishments and their distances from the campus. We then categorized the establishments based on the level of college campus chosen as the origin.


Our project is open-source on Github at https://github.com/KahnAlexander/NAU-CS499-SP. Our data collected from this project is also available there.

Data Visualizations

To analyze our data, we used an online tool called Statkey, which allowed us to easily generate graphs of our results. A single point on one of these graphs represents a single establishment we included in our research. The x-axis of these graphs represents the distance from the particular establishment to the particular campus. The y-axis categorizes the establishment as fast food, grocery, or sitdown. These are preliminary results and do not represent the final product of our research. However, they do provide a solid model for what we expect to find in the finalized version of our project.
